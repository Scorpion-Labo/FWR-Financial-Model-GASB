Overview of the financial statements

This discussion introduces FutureWorld Robotics' basic financial statements, presented as an enterprise fund using the economic resources measurement focus and the accrual basis of accounting. The basic statements are the Statement of Net Position, the Statement of Revenues, Expenses, and Changes in Net Position, and the Statement of Cash Flows, accompanied by notes.

Financial position and results of operations

Total net position grows each year of the projection, from $26.8M at the end of 2027 to $95.0M by 2031, reflecting positive changes in net position in every year. Year 2027 includes a $16.0M capital contribution that establishes the fund. Operating revenues rise from $35.5M (2027) to $50.8M (2031) as attendance ramps and the membership and business lines mature, while operating income remains strongly positive throughout. Because a governmental enterprise fund is tax-exempt, results are reported without income tax.

Capital asset and debt administration

Capital assets, net of accumulated depreciation, are $15.5M at the end of 2027 and $11.4M by 2031, reflecting initial build-out and fleet investment of $18.6M in 2027 followed by annual refresh capital spending net of depreciation. Long-term debt is a $6.0M placeholder facility carried throughout the projection; in a governmental structure this would typically be issued as tax-exempt revenue bonds, with interest reported within capital and related financing activities. Net investment in capital assets (capital assets net of related debt) declines from $9.5M to $5.4M as assets depreciate.

Economic factors and outlook

The projection assumes a five-year price-skim curve and an attendance ramp from 165,000 to 270,000 annual visits. Attendance and willingness-to-pay are the most sensitive inputs; the model's A/B/C scenario set tests downside cases that remain cash-positive. Figures are illustrative and should be validated against local market data.

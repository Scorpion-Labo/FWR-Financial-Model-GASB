Entity & Legal Framework

Gated

Two structural decisions to resolve before the raise closes: the legal entity form, and how to source robots from China without regulatory risk or jeopardizing any affiliated defense business.

Recommended direction (to confirm with counsel)

A governmental enterprise-fund structure: FutureWorld Robotics is operated as a tax-exempt public enterprise fund — for example an enterprise of, or affiliated with, a public university or public authority — capitalized through a capital contribution and tax-exempt revenue bonds, paired with an affiliated nonprofit education foundation eligible for grants and donations. This keeps the commercial operation tax-exempt and self-supporting while opening grant funding through the foundation.

China sourcing & the defense firewall

🏛️

CFIUS

Keep the China relationship as an arm's-length supplier arrangement — not equity. Chinese ownership in the cap table can trigger CFIUS review, under heightened scrutiny for China and AI/robotics.

🛡️

Defense firewall (1260H)

Strict legal and supply-chain separation between FutureWorld Robotics and any affiliated defense work. Screen suppliers against the DoD Section 1260H and restricted-party lists.

📦

Tariffs (Section 301)

Budget Section 301 tariffs on imported robots (7.5%–100% by category). Explore the USTR exclusion process and consider U.S. final assembly.

Questions for counsel

Which entity form best supports a tax-exempt governmental enterprise fund, the public-partner and founder arrangements, and grant eligibility — and in which state? Should education sit in a separate 501(c)(3) foundation? How should the co-founder's robotics-company relationship be documented to protect its DoD eligibility? Does any contemplated Chinese capital trigger CFIUS? Are intended suppliers clear of the 1260H and restricted-party lists? What is the tariff classification and exclusion strategy for the imported fleet?

Next steps

  • Engage corporate/tax counsel to form the entity (and foundation) before the raise
  • Engage trade/national-security counsel on CFIUS, 1260H & tariffs before signing China agreements
  • Document the co-founder's robotics-company relationship and firewall in writing

Note: this organizes the decisions and options to weigh; it is not legal advice. Final structure should be confirmed with qualified corporate, tax, and trade counsel before the raise closes.

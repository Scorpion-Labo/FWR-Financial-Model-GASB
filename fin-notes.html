Note 1 — Reporting entity & basis of accounting

FutureWorld Robotics is presented as an enterprise fund / business-type activity. The financial statements are prepared on the economic resources measurement focus and the accrual basis of accounting, in conformity with U.S. GAAP as prescribed by the Governmental Accounting Standards Board (GASB).

Note 2 — Operating vs. nonoperating

Operating revenues result from exchange transactions (admissions, on-site spend, memberships, education, booths, events). Operating expenses include the cost of providing services and depreciation. Grants (nonexchange), investment income, interest expense, and capital contributions are reported as nonoperating or below the operating line.

Note 3 — Capital assets

Capital assets (robots, build-out, AV/IT) are recorded at cost and depreciated straight-line over estimated useful lives: robots 4 years, build-out 15 years, AV/IT 5 years. See the CapEx & Depreciation schedule for the per-vintage roll-forward carried into this model.

Note 4 — Long-term debt

Long-term debt is a $6.0M placeholder facility (interest-only at 8%). In a governmental structure this would typically be issued as lower-rate tax-exempt revenue bonds; interest is reported within capital & related financing activities.

Note 5 — Net position

Net position is displayed in three components: net investment in capital assets (capital assets net of related debt), restricted, and unrestricted.

Note 6 — Items pending (GASB 68/75/87/96)

Net pension and OPEB liabilities (GASB 68/75), lease right-to-use assets and liabilities (GASB 87), and subscription-based IT arrangements (GASB 96), with related deferred outflows/inflows of resources, are presented as captioned placeholders pending actuarial and contract inputs.

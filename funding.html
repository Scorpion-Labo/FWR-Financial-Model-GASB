The Ask & Use of Funds

Gated · Most sensitive

FutureWorld Robotics is capitalized with $22 million (a $16M capital contribution + $6M tax-exempt revenue bonds) to fund the build-out, robot fleet, systems, and launch through the Christmas 2026 soft opening.

The capital carries the venture from financing close to a cash-generating operation: the model projects a positive change in net position from the first full operating year (2027) and roughly $98 million of cumulative net cash from operating activities over the first five years.

Capital & sustainability (illustrative)

As a governmental enterprise fund, the center has no private ownership, equity, or valuation; the $16M is a capital contribution, not an investment for return. Operating results are strong — income before capital contributions near $10.8M in the first year, rising toward $19.9M by year five — building unrestricted net position and comfortably covering debt service.

Sustainability & growth

Operating surpluses are reinvested in the mission and used to service the revenue bonds. Growth — additional locations, format licensing, franchising and traveling exhibits — would be financed through further capital contributions or tax-exempt debt rather than equity. There is no equity exit.

Financial highlights (illustrative)

  • Cumulative op. cash flow (5yr) ~$98M
  • Yr-5 change in net position ~$19.9M
  • Debt service ~$480k/yr
  • Covered by operating income of $11M+ from Year 1

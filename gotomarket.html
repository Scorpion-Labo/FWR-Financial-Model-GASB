Go-to-Market & Marketing

Public-safe

Marketing effort follows the revenue model: a consumer-led engine (≈89% of Year-1 revenue) with a focused, scaling B2B track.

This plan is built directly from the revenue model: spend follows where revenue comes from. The model is overwhelmingly attendance-driven — gate, on-site, memberships, and education make up about 89% of Year-1 revenue. Business-to-business revenue is smaller (~11%) but higher-margin and the fastest-growing segment. The strategy is consumer-led, with a focused and scaling B2B track.

Acquisition economics

The Year-1 budget implies roughly $15 in marketing per visit ($2.5M ÷ 165,000) — about 7% of the ~$217 blended revenue per visit. That is an efficient ratio for a premium attraction and a defensible anchor for the plan.

Phased launch

Pre-opening brand and founding-membership presale; a grand-opening media surge; an always-on ramp with content-engine reveals; then a steady-state retention focus with new-robot reveals driving repeat visits.

Target segments

  • Tourists OTAs, concierge, tourism boards
  • Local families & members digital, founding drive
  • Schools & education district outreach
  • Business direct B2B sales

Phased launch plan

Pre-opening (T-6)

Build demand before the doors open

Brand and website live; founding-membership presale; OTA/concierge and tourism-board contracting; PR build and influencer seeding; exhibitor and sponsor pre-sales.

Launch (opening)

The grand-opening surge

Media preview day; grand-opening campaign; paid-media surge; launch PR around the “first AI center” story.

Ramp (months 1–12)

Always-on demand and conversion

Always-on digital; content-engine reveals; membership drive; school outreach; active B2B selling.

Steady state (Yr 2+)

Retention and expansion

Retention focus; new-robot reveals to drive repeat; B2B expansion; traveling-exhibit licensing.

Secondary engine — business (B2B)

B2B is smaller today but the fastest-growing and highest-margin segment, acquired through direct selling rather than consumer media: exhibitor and sponsor sales (strongest near the convention calendar — e.g., CES if Las Vegas), corporate events and private hire, and CES-style conference hosting. It is resourced through the commercial team carried in payroll, not the consumer marketing budget.

KPIs tracked

  • Gate paid visits, channel mix, ~$15 cost/visit
  • Membership conversion, renewal rate
  • On-site per-capita spend
  • B2B booths sold, sponsorship $, pipeline
  • Brand earned-media reach, web conversion
